## TONE & STYLE GUIDELINES

### Voice
- **Detective noir**: Dramatic but factual
- **Expert authority**: Professional, confident
- **German formal**: "Sie" form, professional tone
- **Investigative**: Questions, theories, evidence

### Language
- **Active voice**: "Die Täter stahlen" not "wurde gestohlen"
- **Specific numbers**: "€850 Millionen" not "viel Geld"
- **Technical precision**: Name systems, methods, technologies
- **Dramatic but credible**: Tension without melodrama

### Formatting
- **Bold for emphasis**: Key terms, findings
- **Italics for quotes**: Expert statements, dramatic emphasis
- **Lists for clarity**: Evidence, timelines, specifications
- **Short paragraphs**: 2-4 sentences max (readability)

## RESEARCH ELEMENTS TO INCLUDE

### Every Article Should Have:
1. **Specific numbers**: Dates, times, amounts, measurements
2. **Named experts**: "Klaus Weber, Bona Fides" or "Dr. Sophie Laurent, Louvre"
3. **Technical details**: System names, specifications, protocols
4. **Timeline elements**: Sequence of events, duration
5. **Comparative data**: Historical context, similar cases
6. **Open questions**: What remains unknown

### Evidence Types:
- **Physical**: DNA, fingerprints, fibers, materials
- **Digital**: Malware, logs, communications, metadata
- **Documentary**: Records, protocols, reports, memos
- **Testimonial**: Witnesses, experts, officials
- **Circumstantial**: Patterns, anomalies, connections

## SEO OPTIMIZATION

### Keywords:
- **Primary**: In title, first paragraph, H2 headings
- **Secondary**: 3-5 related terms throughout
- **Natural integration**: No keyword stuffing
- **German variations**: Synonyms, related terms

### Internal Linking:
- **3-4 links** to related articles
- **Anchor text**: Descriptive, keyword-rich
- **Related series**: Link within same series

### External Authority:
- Reference real institutions (Interpol, Louvre, FBI)
- Link to official sources (when credible)
- Build topical authority
